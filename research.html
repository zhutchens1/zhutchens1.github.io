Research

I am an observational astrophysicist interested in understanding how galaxies like our own Milky Way form and evolve over cosmic time. To probe galaxy evolution, I apply data science methods to multiple galaxy surveys, which are like databases of galaxies that contain measurements of the properties (e.g., brightness, color, mass) of thousands of galaxies. By applying statistical methods to these large datasets, we uncover features and relationships about the population of galaxies in the Universe, thus allowing us to understand the astrophysical processes that drive galaxy evolution and to place constraints on theoretical predictions.

Identifying Galaxy Groups in the Nearby and Distant Universe



Many galaxies are not isolated but rather live in groups or clusters where they interact with other galaxies. To probe such interactions and the properties of groups, we must first identify galaxy groups. As a graduate student, I led the development of the Gas in Galaxy Groups (G3) group-finding algorithm, conceptualized above, a computational approach to identifying groups based on galaxy coordinates and positions. This algorithm improves on prior work because it enables reliable group identification even in incomplete surveys, i.e., galaxy surveys that lack a complete accounting of all galaxies. The G3 algorithm also offers superior group-finding performance compared to the traditional "friends-of-friends" technique.

The HI-Halo Mass Relation in the Local Universe

The "HI - halo mass relation" is an important statistic in galaxy evolution that has been widely examined in recent observational and theoretical work. The relation, pictured above, describes the distribution of neutral atomic hydrogen (HI) gas in dark matter halos in the Universe. Since HI is the primary gaseous component of galaxies like the Milky Way, and because galaxies and groups evolve in dark matter halos, the HI-halo mass relation probes numerous astrophysical processes. In 2023, we used the RESOLVE and ECO surveys to provide a robust measurement of the relation. While prior measurements were based on indirect inference or spectral stacking (which conceals scatter), we provided a direct measurement of its shape and scatter using the comprehensive galaxy HI data offered by RESOLVE and ECO.
